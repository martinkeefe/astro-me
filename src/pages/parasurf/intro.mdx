## Introduction

A parametric surface is a 3-dimensional surface defined by a set of
parametric equations with two parameters. These equations map points
$(u,v)$ in some 2-dimensional space $D$ (the _domain_) to the
position vectors of the points on the 3-dimensional surface $S$. This
parametric representation can be written:


  > $\vec{r}(u,v) \:=\: x(u,v)\,\vec{i} + y(u,v)\,\vec{j} + z(u,v)\,\vec{k}$


The vectors $\vec{i}$, $\vec{j}$ and $\vec{k}$ are the [basis
vectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basis_%28linear_algebra%29)
of the 3-dimensional space that the surface is embedded in. For
Cartesian coordinates in the normal [Euclidean space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_space)
$\mathbb{R}^3$ these are the familiar X, Y and Z axes.

For example, the parametric representation of a cone is:

  > $\vec{r}(u,v) \:=\: u\cos v\,\vec{i} + u\sin v\,\vec{j} + u\,\vec{k}$

So the parametric equations are:

  > $x \:=\: u\cos v$ <br/>
  > $y \:=\: u\sin v$ <br/>
  > $z \:=\: u$

And the domain is:

  > $u \:\in\: \mathbb{R}$ <br />
  > $v \;\in\: \mathbb{R}, \; -\pi \leq v \lt \pi$
